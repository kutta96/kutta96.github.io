Fly over the Lake

Brief Introduction

I am crazy about various kinds of sky and clouds. Light travels in atmosphere, frequently colliding with air molecules (in smaller scale) and other particles (in larger scale). Although I know their mechanism, I am still eager to create them myself.

Shadertoy shows its magic and power when I firstly visited the website. Without repeatedly writing many common functions and pipeline, the website makes us pay more attention to the shader language (GLSL) and enjoy its magic. After learning basics of shader language and completing exercises, I tried to make a small project to improve my skills.

Scratchapixel and The Book of Shaders provide excellent lessons and technical details regarding atmospheric scattering, which helps me a lot. In addition, Shicz86 and w23 presented the excellent work on these topics and also helped me. Note that the cloud is from noise functions instead of 3D noise textures.

Features

  • Ray marching
  • Rayleigh scattering
  • Mie scattering
  • Volumetric clouds
  • Fractal Brownian motion
  • Eye fish camera model

Results

When you observe the video, it seems that you are flying over the lake. In order to get the correct reflection of the lake, the camera is under the water and reflected picture is not strictly symmetrical about the lake since you are flying at a certain altitude from the lake.

When dealing with sky simulation using ray marching method, Mie scattering and Rayleigh scattering strictly obey the physical rules, but the correct result depends on the steps of the ray-marching process. In other words, the discretization of the equation should be correctly made. Large integral step may make it difficult to converge and then lead to energy loss.
