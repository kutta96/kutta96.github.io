Spectrometry and Interferometry

Brief Introduction

The interpretation of spectrum[1] would provide us with much information about temperature, particle density and state transformation. Emission spectroscopy would not disturb objects' states. Based on optically thin assumption, the emission spectroscopy could be an appropriate technique to detect objects.
Interferometry is suitable for some cases where the information of the whole region needs to be known. Two-color Mach-Zehnder interferometer[2] is more useful since it could decouple different components and simultaneously measure various parameters.

Features

  • High spatial-temporal resolution
  • Radial parameter reconstruction
  • Fast system calibration
  • Decoupling of parameters

Results

Abel inversion is necessary for data interpretation. We could see the difference between Fitted and Fitted & Inverted.

Abel inversion is also needed when interpretating data from the two-color Mach-Zehnder interferometer. In addition, some algorithms are important.

  • First, the image is transferred into the gray image, and the frequency of interference fringes is selected by FFT to eliminate the noise
  • Second, the two-dimensional wrapped phase distribution is obtained
  • Third, the phase is unwrapped using the algorithm of adjacent branch cutting
  • Finally, subtracting the unwrapped phase distribution at a certain time by the initial unwrapped phase at time0, the phase shift distribution is obtained
