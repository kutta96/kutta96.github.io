Laser Optics and Data Augmentation

Brief Introduction

The laser-induced plasma technology is applied to create highly reproducible plasmas, and the Thomson scattering is used to measure the electron number density and electron temperature. The data augmentation of the Thomson scattering ensures the quality of spectral data and the reliability of results. The temporal resolution is 15ns and the spatial resolution is 78μm[1].

Features

  • High spatial-temporal resolution
  • Separation of various scattering signals
  • Multiple optical detection methods
  • Accurate data fitting and analysis
  • Data smoothing and augmentation

Our target signal is Thomson scattering signal, but unluckily, it is extremely weak compared to other signals such as Mie scattering, Rayleigh scattering and self-emission spectrum. The figure illustrates a typical result.

Results

The target signal can be observed and changes with time. First, signals are symmetric about the central wavelength 532nm. As the decay goes, they are closer to the central wavelength.

Meanwhile, the data augmentation excludes massive unrelated signals and extract the weak target signal. Based on a large precomputed table, the data fitting program solves spectral data in high spatial resolution.
